Synopsis

Xie Lian and Hua Cheng recount their recent adventures during a quiet night at Puqi Shrine. Amidst the serenity, the Crimson Rain Sought Flower reveals a little more about Xie Lian's true character, and an embarrassing memory reminds him of how much he has changed since his previous ascensions. However, no matter how much time has passed, Xie Lian never fails to believe in the good he sees in the people around him. Though the heavens may ridicule him, his words and actions have earned him the most devout of followers.

[Written by MAL Rewrite]

Edit

Background

Heaven Official's Blessing (, Tian Guan Ci Fu) is a donghua based on a Chinese web novel of the same name, written by Mo Xiang Tong Xiu (). The show falls into the xianxia genre (a fantasy genre influence by Taoism, Buddhism, and Chinese mythology).

This special is a direct continuation to the 12 episode Tian Guan Ci Fu series. It is a slower paced episode based on character development and oh boy, does it do this in spades. Watching this was like watching a flower start to bloom, petal by petal, unfurling in slow motion. Conversations fully laden with meaning. Restraint. The slow unveiling of a mystery. And then ... the montage with the revelation that you might or might not miss towards the end, is like the flower blooming in full. I was stunned at the beauty and the realization of what it meant.

I had debated whether I should give this a 10 because it is only one episode. The slow unveil leading to a climax has rarely been done half as well in other series. This might be the best one I can recall in recent anime watching history. The face reveal, I had to make sure and rewind it several times to re-watch it. My emotions were in a roil after (I have not read the novel). It makes sense, my heart was singing at the "rightness" of it. If anything can generate this kind of feeling and emotion, it deserves a 10.

Its brave but dumb to have such ambitions when you were young. Its dumb, but also brave

- Hua Cheng

This special episode is a really nice way to wrap up the first series of Tian Guan Ci Fu, focusing more on the relationship between the two main characters and acting as a tie-up for the main plot threads that were started.

The more relaxed nature of this episode really highlights the strengths of the series, most notably the animation style and the music which are allowed to shine a little more than in the main episodes. The main issue with this special is that, due to its focus on concluding what has happened, it can feel a little bit repetitive. However, it doesnt affect the rest of the episode and it is an understandable necessity.

This episode is definitely a great way to wrap up the series and makes me hopeful for the next season.
